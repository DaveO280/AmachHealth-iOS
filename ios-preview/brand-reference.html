Step 1 — Choose your mark

Symbol Language Options

Celtic Knotwork

The letterform is drawn as a single continuous interlaced line — no start, no end. Represents the unbroken cycle of health, time, and nature.

→ The 'A' in Amach becomes the knot. Highly ownable, unmistakably Celtic.

Triskelion

Ireland's oldest symbol — carved at Newgrange 5,000 years ago. Three spirals in perpetual motion: mind · body · spirit. Used as the icon mark beside a clean wordmark.

→ Standalone icon + "Amach Health" wordmark in Cinzel serif. Classic lockup.

Ogham Script

The ancient Irish tree-alphabet, written on standing stones. Vertical stem with horizontal/diagonal notches. The geometry of Ogham informs abstract letterform proportions.

→ The 'A' takes on vertical proportions and deliberate stroke geometry. Minimal, architectural.

Sacred Geometry

Flower of Life — universal pattern found in ancient Celtic art, Tibetan temples, and DNA structure. The proportions govern the letterform. Science meets spirit.

→ Logo built on golden ratio grid. Clean, precise letterforms with hidden geometric meaning.
